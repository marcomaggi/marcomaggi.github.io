78.3.3 Low level functions

The following bindings are exported by the (nausicaa standard-numbers verhoeff low) library.

— Function: sequence->check-digit getter

Compute and return the check digit for a sequence of fixnums in the range [0, 9]. getter must be a thunk returning the sequence of fixnums one at a time and returning #f when no more fixnums are available. 

          #!r6rs
          (import (nausicaa)
            (nausicaa standard-numbers verhoeff low))
          
          (define (make-list-getter sequence)
            (lambda ()
              (and (not (null? sequence))
                   (begin0
                       (car sequence)
                     (set! sequence (cdr sequence))))))
          
          (sequence->check-digit
            (make-list-getter '(7 5 8 2 4 1)))    ⇒ 0
          
          (sequence->check-digit
            (make-list-getter '(4 5 6)))          ⇒ 8
          
          (sequence->check-digit
            (make-list-getter '(9 9 9)))          ⇒ 8
— Function: char->digit char
— Function: digit->char digit

Convert between characters in the alphabet and digit fixnums in the range [0, 9]. An assertion violation is raised if the character is out of range.

— Function: fixnum->digit fixnum
— Function: digit->fixnum digit

Convert between fixnums and digit fixnums in the range [0, 9]; each fixnum is meant to represent a character from the alphabet in ASCII encoding (such fixnums can be produced by applying char->integer to the characters). An assertion violation is raised if the fixnum is out of range.
