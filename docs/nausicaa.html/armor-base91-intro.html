63.5.1 Introduction to base91 encoding

This encoding differs from base32 and base64 in that:

  • The output length depends upon the input data.
  • There is no padding.
  • The encoder/decoder context buffers input data.

The overhead produced by base91 depends on the input data: the encoder outputs 16 bits of ASCII characters every 13 or 14 bits of binary input. The overhead amounts at most to 23\% (versus 33\% for base64) and can range down to 14\%, which typically occurs on zero–byte blocks.

As the name suggests, base91 needs 91 characters to represent the encoded binary data in ASCII; from the 94 printable ASCII characters (‘#x21’..‘#x7E’), the following three have been omitted to build the base 91 alphabet: 

     #\-     dash, 0x2D
     #\'     apostrophe, 0x27
     #\\     backslash, 0x5C

the alphabet is composed of the remaining characters: 

     A B C   D E F   G H I   J    ;;  0 -  9
     K L M   N O P   Q R S   T    ;; 10 - 19
     U V W   X Y Z   a b c   d    ;; 20 - 29
     e f g   h i j   k l m   n    ;; 30 - 39
     o p q   r s t   u v w   x    ;; 40 - 49
     y z 0   1 2 3   4 5 6   7    ;; 50 - 59
     8 9 !   # $ %   & ( )   *    ;; 60 - 69
     + , .   / : ;   < = >   ?    ;; 70 - 79
     @ [ ]   ^ _ `   { | }   ~    ;; 80 - 89
     "                            ;; 90
