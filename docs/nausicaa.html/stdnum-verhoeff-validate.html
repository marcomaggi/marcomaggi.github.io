75.2.3 Validating identification numbers

To validate a sequence of digits against a given check digit: we compute again the check digit then compare it to the given one. The following bindings are exported by the (nausicaa standard-numbers verhoeff) library.

— Function: verhoeff-number? integer

Return #t if the exact integer number integer is valid according to the Verhoeff checksum. 

          (verhoeff-number? 1428570)      ⇒ #t
          (verhoeff-number? 1428571)      ⇒ #f
— Function: verhoeff-list? ell

Given a list ell of fixnums in the range [0, 9], return #t if it is valid according to the Verhoeff checksum. The car of ell must be the check digit and the cdr must be the sequence of digits starting from its car. No validation is performed on the input list. 

          (verhoeff-list? '(0 7 5 8 2 4 1))       ⇒ #t
          (verhoeff-list? '(1 7 5 8 2 4 1))       ⇒ #f
— Function: verhoeff-string? str

Given a string str of digits in the range [0, 9], return #t if it is valid according to the Verhoeff checksum. The last character in str must represent the check digit, the rest of the string must be the sequence of digits starting from the rightmost character. No validation is performed on the input string. 

          (verhoeff-string? "1428570")    ⇒ #t
          (verhoeff-string? "1428571")    ⇒ #f
— Function: verhoeff-vector-left? v
— Function: verhoeff-vector-right? v

Given a vector v of fixnums in the range [0, 9], return #t if it is valid according to the Verhoeff checksum. No validation is performed on the input vector.

verhoeff-vector-left? interprets the first element of v as the check digit and the rest of v as a sequence of digits starting from the leftmost element.

verhoeff-vector-right? interprets the last element of v as the check digit and the rest of v as a sequence of digits starting from the rightmost element. 

          (verhoeff-vector-left? '#(0 7 5 8 2 4 1))       ⇒ #t
          (verhoeff-vector-left? '#(1 7 5 8 2 4 1))       ⇒ #f
          
          (verhoeff-vector-right? '#(1 4 2 8 5 7 0))      ⇒ #t
          (verhoeff-vector-right? '#(1 4 2 8 5 7 1))      ⇒ #f
— Function: verhoeff-bytevector-left? bv
— Function: verhoeff-bytevector-right? bv

Given a bytevector bv of bytes in the range [0, 9], return #t if it is valid according to the Verhoeff checksum. No validation is performed on the input bytevector.

verhoeff-bytevector-left? interprets the first byte of bv as the check digit and the rest of bv as a sequence of digits starting from the leftmost byte.

verhoeff-bytevector-right? interprets the last byte of bv as the check digit and the rest of bv as a sequence of digits starting from the rightmost byte. 

          (verhoeff-bytevector-left? '#vu8(0 7 5 8 2 4 1))   ⇒ #t
          (verhoeff-bytevector-left? '#vu8(1 7 5 8 2 4 1))   ⇒ #f
          
          (verhoeff-bytevector-right? '#vu8(1 4 2 8 5 7 0))  ⇒ #t
          (verhoeff-bytevector-right? '#vu8(1 4 2 8 5 7 1))  ⇒ #f
