44.1.2 International Atomic Time (TAI)

A realisation of Terrestrial Time is the International Atomic Time, which is based on the notional passage of proper time on Earth's surface.

Time coordinates on the TAI scale are expressed using the SI seconds and the time coordinates defined by the proleptic Gregorian calendar. Gregorian calendar.

The timestamp ‘2010-08-05T11:22:33Z’ is valid on the TAI scale with the usual count of years, month and days on the Gregorian calendar. The count of seconds is always in the range [0, 59] because the TAI scale has no leap seconds.
