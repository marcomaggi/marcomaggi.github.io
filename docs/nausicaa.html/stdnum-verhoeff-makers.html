75.2.2 Composing identification numbers

The following bindings are exported by the (nausicaa standard-numbers verhoeff) library.

— Function: number->verhoeff integer

Compute the check digit for the exact integer number integer and return a new exact integer number representing integer with the checksum added: 

          (+ (* 10 integer) check)

Examples: 

          (number->verhoeff 142857)       ⇒ 1428570
          (number->verhoeff 654)          ⇒ 6548
          (number->verhoeff 999)          ⇒ 9998
— Function: list->verhoeff ell

Given a list ell of fixnums in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return a list having the check digit as car and ell itself as cdr; the list is interpreted as a sequence of digits starting from its car. No validation is performed on the input list. 

          (list->verhoeff '(7 5 8 2 4 1)) ⇒ (0 7 5 8 2 4 1)
          (list->verhoeff '(4 5 6))       ⇒ (8 4 5 6)
          (list->verhoeff '(9 9 9))       ⇒ (8 9 9 9)
— Function: string->verhoeff str

Given a string str of digits in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return str with the check digit appended; the string is interpreted as sequence of digits starting from the rightmost character. No validation is performed on the input string. 

          (string->verhoeff "142857")     ⇒ "1428570"
          (string->verhoeff "654")        ⇒ "6548"
          (string->verhoeff "999")        ⇒ "9998"
— Function: vector->left-verhoeff v
— Function: vector->right-verhoeff v

Given a vector v of fixnums in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return v with the check digit prepended or appended. No validation is performed on the input vector.

vector->left-verhoeff considers v as a sequence of digits starting from the leftmost element, so it returns a vector with the check digit prepended.

vector->right-verhoeff considers v as a sequence of digits starting from the rightmost element, so it returns a vector with the check digit appended. 

          (vector->left-verhoeff '#(7 5 8 2 4 1)) ⇒ #(0 7 5 8 2 4 1)
          (vector->left-verhoeff '#(4 5 6))       ⇒ #(8 4 5 6)
          (vector->left-verhoeff '#(9 9 9))       ⇒ #(8 9 9 9)
          
          (vector->right-verhoeff '#(1 4 2 8 5 7)) ⇒ #(1 4 2 8 5 7 0)
          (vector->right-verhoeff '#(6 5 4))       ⇒ #(6 5 4 8)
          (vector->right-verhoeff '#(9 9 9))       ⇒ #(9 9 9 8)
— Function: bytevector->left-verhoeff bv
— Function: bytevector->right-verhoeff bv

Given a bytevector bv of bytes in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return bv with the check digit prepended or appended. No validation is performed on the input bytevector.

bytevector->left-verhoeff considers bv as a sequence of digits starting from the leftmost byte, so it returns a bytevector with the check digit prepended.

bytevector->right-verhoeff considers bv as a sequence of digits starting from the rightmost byte, so it returns a bytevector with the check digit appended. 

          (bytevector->left-verhoeff '#vu8(7 5 8 2 4 1))
          ⇒ #vu8(0 7 5 8 2 4 1)
          (bytevector->left-verhoeff '#vu8(4 5 6))
          ⇒ #vu8(8 4 5 6)
          (bytevector->left-verhoeff '#vu8(9 9 9))
          ⇒ #vu8(8 9 9 9)
          
          (bytevector->right-verhoeff '#vu8(1 4 2 8 5 7))
          ⇒ #vu8(1 4 2 8 5 7 0)
          (bytevector->right-verhoeff '#vu8(6 5 4))
          ⇒ #vu8(6 5 4 8)
          (bytevector->right-verhoeff '#vu8(9 9 9))
          ⇒ #vu8(9 9 9 8)
