75.1.3 Composing identification numbers

The following bindings are exported by the (nausicaa standard-numbers luhn) library.

— Function: number->luhn10 integer

Compute the check digit for the exact integer number integer and return a new exact integer number representing integer with the checksum added: 

          (+ (* 10 integer) check)

Examples: 

          (number->luhn10 54328)          ⇒ 543280
          (number->luhn10 4992739871)     ⇒ 49927398716
          (number->luhn10 302)            ⇒ 3020
          (number->luhn10 312)            ⇒ 3129
— Function: list->luhn10 ell

Given a list ell of fixnums in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return a list having the check digit as car and ell itself as cdr; the list is interpreted as a sequence of digits starting from its car. No validation is performed on the input list. 

          (list->luhn10 '(2 0 3)) ⇒ (0 2 0 3)
          (list->luhn10 '(2 1 3)) ⇒ (9 2 1 3)
— Function: string->luhn10 str

Given a string str of digits in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return str with the check digit appended; the string is interpreted as sequence of digits starting from the rightmost character. No validation is performed on the input string. 

          (string->luhn10 "54328")        ⇒ "543280"
          (string->luhn10 "4992739871")   ⇒ "49927398716"
          (string->luhn10 "302")          ⇒ "3020"
          (string->luhn10 "312")          ⇒ "3129"
— Function: vector->left-luhn10 v
— Function: vector->right-luhn10 v

Given a vector v of fixnums in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return v with the check digit prepended or appended. No validation is performed on the input vector.

vector->left-luhn10 considers v as a sequence of digits starting from the leftmost element, so it returns a vector with the check digit prepended.

vector->right-luhn10 considers v as a sequence of digits starting from the rightmost element, so it returns a vector with the check digit appended. 

          (vector->left-luhn10 '#(8 2 3 4 5))
          ⇒ #(0 8 2 3 4 5)
          
          (vector->left-luhn10 '#(1 7 8 9 3 7 2 9 9 4))
          ⇒ #(6 1 7 8 9 3 7 2 9 9 4)
          
          (vector->left-luhn10 '#(2 0 3))
          ⇒ #(0 2 0 3)
          
          (vector->left-luhn10 '#(2 1 3))
          ⇒ #(9 2 1 3)
          
          (vector->right-luhn10 '#(5 4 3 2 8))
          ⇒ #(5 4 3 2 8 0)
          
          (vector->right-luhn10 '#(4 9 9 2 7 3 9 8 7 1))
          ⇒ #(4 9 9 2 7 3 9 8 7 1 6)
          
          (vector->right-luhn10 '#(3 0 2))
          ⇒ #(3 0 2 0)
          
          (vector->right-luhn10 '#(3 1 2))
          ⇒ #(3 1 2 9)
— Function: bytevector->left-luhn10 bv
— Function: bytevector->right-luhn10 bv

Given a bytevector bv of bytes in the range [0, 9], compute the check digit and return bv with the check digit prepended or appended. No validation is performed on the input bytevector.

bytevector->left-luhn10 considers bv as a sequence of digits starting from the leftmost byte, so it returns a bytevector with the check digit prepended.

bytevector->right-luhn10 considers bv as a sequence of digits starting from the rightmost byte, so it returns a bytevector with the check digit appended. 

          (bytevector->left-luhn10 '#vu8(8 2 3 4 5))
          ⇒ #vu8(0 8 2 3 4 5)
          
          (bytevector->left-luhn10 '#vu8(1 7 8 9 3 7 2 9 9 4))
          ⇒ #vu8(6 1 7 8 9 3 7 2 9 9 4)
          
          (bytevector->left-luhn10 '#vu8(2 0 3))
          ⇒ #vu8(0 2 0 3)
          
          (bytevector->left-luhn10 '#vu8(2 1 3))
          ⇒ #vu8(9 2 1 3)
          
          (bytevector->right-luhn10 '#vu8(5 4 3 2 8))
          ⇒ #vu8(5 4 3 2 8 0)
          
          (bytevector->right-luhn10 '#vu8(4 9 9 2 7 3 9 8 7 1))
          ⇒ #vu8(4 9 9 2 7 3 9 8 7 1 6)
          
          (bytevector->right-luhn10 '#vu8(3 0 2))
          ⇒ #vu8(3 0 2 0)
          
          (bytevector->right-luhn10 '#vu8(3 1 2))
          ⇒ #vu8(3 1 2 9)
