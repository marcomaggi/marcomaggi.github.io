32.2.3 Cryptographically secure PRNG

A cryptographically secure pseudo–random number generator (CSPRNG) is a PRNG with properties that make it suitable for use in cryptography. The requirements of an ordinary PRNG are also satisfied by a CSPRNG, but the reverse is not true.

CSPRNG requirements fall into two groups: that they pass statistical randomness tests; that they hold up well under serious attacks, even when part of their initial or running state becomes available to an attacker1.
